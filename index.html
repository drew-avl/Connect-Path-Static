What the IT review produces

A usable map of your environment—not a sales checklist.

The first job is to make the environment legible. We organize the findings around evidence, ownership, and a sensible order of work.

  1. 01

    Access register

    The register identifies administrator roles, MFA coverage, recovery access, and the owner of each critical account.

  2. 02

    Asset baseline

    The baseline documents computers, network equipment, business systems, and devices that fall outside the standard.

  3. 03

    Recovery check

    The check verifies what is backed up, what each restore depends on, and which recovery steps still need an owner.

  4. 04

    Vendor map

    The map records providers, account owners, support paths, and the handoffs that tend to stall when something breaks.

  5. 05

    Priority register

    The register places immediate risks, recurring problems, and longer-term improvements in a practical order.

Before we start

A few practical questions.

Every environment has its own history. These are a few questions we can answer without turning the conversation into a pitch. For anything specific, call (828) 515-1530.

What happens during an IT review?

We look at access, devices, Microsoft 365, networks, security controls, backups, recurring issues, and vendor dependencies. We organize the findings into what needs attention now, what should come next, and what can wait.

Can ConnectPath work with the vendors we already use?

Yes. A line-of-business application, copier company, carrier, or software vendor can remain part of the environment. ConnectPath documents the support path, coordinates the handoff, and keeps the next step visible.

Do we have to change internet providers?

No. Connectivity is one available service, not the center of the relationship. We review an existing connection when it affects the business, and we compare available providers when a change would benefit the business.

What if we are switching IT providers?

We begin by confirming administrator access, checking backups, identifying devices and vendors, and documenting the current support path. That creates a controlled starting point without depending on one person’s memory.
